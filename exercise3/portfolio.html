Modelling urban charging network for electric vehicles
Spring 2020

  • As part of my work in consulting, I was assigned to a project to model a charging network in an urban area. Our task was to answer where in a given urban area can a charger be and what kind of charger would that be. I worked with in a team with three other colleagues. However, my main task was to use QGIS to analyse and model the data.
  • Together we produced a map which shows all the possible locations for an electric vehicle charger. These locations are located in more or less favourable area for installing charging infrastructure which further informs the client whether it is worth building at a certain location.

The Compass Project - “Design, art and craft in Ilica street: The urban laboratory”
Oct 2016 - May 2017, Croatia

  • The proposal was called “Design, art and craft in Ilica street: The urban laboratory”. The area in question was traditionally occupied by creative industries, artists and craftsmen, but due to political changes in the 90s, land value and land use change these activities were becoming difficult to maintain. This resulted in many abandoned locations, low social and economic energy and lack of services for local inhabitants.
  • Our team proposed to revitalize the street by returning to traditional activities and by introducing elements of green infrastructure. This would keep the sense of place while also introducing new social and economic energy to the area.
